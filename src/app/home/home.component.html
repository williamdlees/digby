About VDJbase

VDJbase is a public database of immunoglobulin and T cell receptor genes, genotypes, and haplotypes. Initially developed using adaptive immune receptor repertoire sequencing (AIRR-seq) based inferences, the database now also incorporates growing amounts of information derived from long-read genomic sequencing data.

Comprehensive knowledge of immunoglobulin and T cell receptor genes across human populations and model organisms is critical for understanding immune function and variability. In VDJbase we curate data from hundreds of individuals, enabling the discovery of previously unreported alleles and supporting population-level analysis of immune receptor variation.

The platform offers customizable reports to explore gene and allele usage patterns, based on both AIRR-seq and genomic sources. All data and tools are freely available under an open-source license. Through VDJbase, you can:

  • Explore the AIRR-seq and genomic data available in VDJbase today
  • Browse AIRR-seq-derived datasets and alleles
  • Browse genomically derived datasets and alleles
  • View alignments in an integrated genome browser
  • Generate interactive reports on gene and allele usage
  • Access documentation on reports and a user guide

All features are available via the top menu.

If you use VDJbase in your work, please cite our paper.

VDJbase is developed and maintained by the Yaari Lab at Yale University in collaboration with William Lees. For more information, please contact Gur Yaari or William Lees.
