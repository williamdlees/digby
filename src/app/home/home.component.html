About VDJbase

VDJbase is a database of adaptive immune receptor genes, genotypes and haplotypes. Our core collection is inferred from immune receptor repertoire sequences, as recently described in Nucleic Acids Research. We are also beginning to add genomically derived material.

Detailed knowledge of these genes, in humans and key research species, is vital to the understanding of the immune response. Current reference sets, widely used in immune repertoire research, are often incomplete. Problems in the human and mouse sets are long-standing. VDJbase allows researchers to consult inferences from many hundreds of samples, allowing a population-level understanding of genes and alleles to be developed, and hitherto unidentified alleles to be described.

VDJbase provides customisable reports, which allow users to study gene and allele usage in various ways. Reports, and the data underlying them, are provided under open-source licence. If you use VDJbase in your research, please cite our paper.

From here you can:

  • Explore the data available in VDJbase today
  • Read our user guide or find more information on reports

  • Browse the adaptive immune receptor repertore (AIRR) datasets
  • Browse alleles identified from the AIRR datasets

  • Browse the genomically-derived datasets
  • Browse alleles identified from the genomically-derived datasets
  • View alignments in a genome browser.

All options are also available from the menu at the top of each page.

VDJbase is jointly developed and maintained by members of the Yaari Lab at the Faculty of Engineering at Bar Ilan University, Israel. For further information, please contact Gur Yaari or William Lees.
